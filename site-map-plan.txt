Rook.works Site Map and Portfolio Plan

Goal
- Position the site for remote HR Generalist, HRIS, People Ops, Business Analyst, and internal systems roles.
- Make the site read as a professional portfolio, not a demo catalog.

Primary Message
- I help HR teams run cleaner operations through better process design, documentation, reporting, and lightweight systems.
- Secondary strength: building internal tools and automations that support compliance, training, leave, records, and handoffs.

Target Audience
- HR managers and directors
- People operations and HRIS teams
- Operations leaders
- Hiring managers for HR Generalist and BA-adjacent roles
- Small and mid-size teams that need process cleanup and tools

Recommended Site Structure
1. Home
- Hero with role-specific headline
- Short value proposition
- CTAs: View case studies, Download resume, Contact
- Proof bar with key HR capabilities
- Featured case studies
- Capabilities summary
- About
- Contact

2. Case Studies
- 3 featured portfolio stories only
- Each case study should include:
  - problem
  - context / stakeholders
  - your role
  - tools used
  - solution
  - outcome / measurable impact
- Recommended featured items:
  - Training Attendance Architecture
  - Hexadecaform Intake
  - One internal reporting / workflow automation example

3. Capabilities
- HR operations support
- Leave / FMLA / ADA tracking support
- Offboarding / onboarding workflows
- Training and development coordination
- Time and attendance auditing
- Personnel records and data hygiene
- HRIS / spreadsheet / workflow tooling
- Employee communications and documentation

4. About
- Short, direct bio
- Experience summary
- Types of teams supported
- Why you work in HR systems / ops
- Tool stack and working style

5. Resume
- PDF download or direct page summary
- Keep it easy to find

6. Contact
- Email link
- LinkedIn link
- Optional calendar link
- Short form only if it is actually monitored

7. Archive / Experiments
- Keep creative or unrelated projects here
- Do not lead with these on the homepage
- Examples: worldbuilding, merch, side concepts, visual experiments

Home Page Priority
- Lead with HR, process, and systems
- Show proof fast
- Keep visual polish, but remove anything that feels whimsical or off-domain
- Make the tone reliable, organized, and discreet

Content Direction
- Use terms that match the job lane:
  - compliance
  - training
  - records
  - leave administration
  - offboarding
  - employee relations support
  - HRIS
  - reporting
  - workflow improvement
  - documentation
- Avoid language that feels too game-like, fantasy-heavy, or product-lab experimental on the main site

Design Direction
- Clean, trustworthy, enterprise-leaning
- Strong typography and spacing
- Dark or muted palette is fine if it stays readable and professional
- Visual energy should support credibility, not distract from it

Featured Case Studies
1. Training Attendance Architecture
- Problem: training data was inconsistent, hard to audit, and difficult to export cleanly.
- My role: rebuilt the structure, validation, and reporting flow.
- Outcome: cleaner records, easier review, and a more reliable handoff to the training log.

2. Hexadecaform Intake
- Problem: one intake process had too many manual paths and too much room for errors.
- My role: designed the intake structure and downstream flow model.
- Outcome: a clearer handoff system that reduces confusion and makes requests easier to route.

3. Internal Reporting / Automation Example
- Problem: recurring admin work was taking too much time and depended on manual follow-up.
- My role: mapped the process, identified repeated steps, and built a lighter workflow.
- Outcome: faster processing, better visibility, and less rework.
